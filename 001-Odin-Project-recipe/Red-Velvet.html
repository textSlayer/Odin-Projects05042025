Red velvet cake is known for its vibrant red color and velvety-soft, moist texture. It's uniquely flavored with a hint of cocoa and often paired with tangy cream cheese frosting for a perfect sweet-tart balance.

Description

Red velvet cake is a visually striking dessert known for its rich red hue and soft, tender crumb. Unlike a standard chocolate cake, red velvet contains a small amount of cocoa powder, giving it a subtle chocolate flavor without overpowering sweetness. Its signature color traditionally came from a chemical reaction between natural cocoa and acidic ingredients, though modern recipes often enhance the red with food coloring.

The texture of red velvet is where it truly shines—it’s moist, light, and velvety, thanks to the use of buttermilk and vinegar. These acidic components react with baking soda to create a fluffy rise and distinctive crumb. This combination makes every bite smooth and melt-in-your-mouth, setting it apart from denser cakes.

Red velvet is almost always paired with a creamy, tangy cream cheese frosting, which perfectly balances the cake’s mild cocoa flavor and rich sweetness. It's a popular choice for celebrations, from birthdays to weddings, and its luxurious appearance and taste have made it a timeless classic in the world of desserts.

INGREDIENTS

Dry Ingredients:

  • 2 ½ cups (312g) all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • ½ teaspoon salt

Wet Ingredients:

  • 1 ½ cups (300g) granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon white vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons red food coloring (liquid or gel)
  • 1 cup (240ml) buttermilk
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup (240ml) vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

STEPS

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease two 9-inch round cake pans.
  2. In a bowl, whisk together flour, cocoa powder, baking soda, and salt.
  3. In a separate large bowl, beat the sugar and oil until combined.
  4. Add eggs one at a time to the sugar-oil mixture, beating well after each addition.
  5. Stir in the buttermilk, vanilla extract, vinegar, and red food coloring.
  6. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet mixture, mixing until smooth.
  7. Divide the batter evenly between the prepared pans and smooth the tops.
  8. Bake for 25–30 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean.
  9. Let the cakes cool in the pans for 10 minutes, then transfer them to a wire rack to cool completely.
  10. Meanwhile, prepare the cream cheese frosting by beating cream cheese and butter until fluffy.
  11. Add powdered sugar and vanilla extract to the frosting mixture and beat until smooth.
  12. Once the cakes are completely cool, frost the top of one layer, stack the second layer, and frost the entire cake.
