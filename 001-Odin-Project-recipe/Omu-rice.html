OMU RICE RECIPE

Omurice is a Japanese comfort dish made of fluffy omelet wrapped around or laid over ketchup-flavored fried rice. It’s often drizzled with ketchup or demi-glace sauce and loved for its cozy, homey vibe.

Description

Omurice, short for “omelet rice,” is a beloved Japanese fusion dish that beautifully blends Western and Eastern cuisines. At its core, it features seasoned fried rice—typically cooked with ketchup, chicken, onions, and vegetables—wrapped in a soft, fluffy omelet. Often shaped like a football or topped with a delicately folded omelet, it’s both visually charming and incredibly satisfying to eat.

The magic of omurice lies in its versatility and comforting flavor. The ketchup-based rice delivers a tangy, slightly sweet taste, while the omelet adds a creamy richness that balances the dish. Some versions are topped with cheese, demi-glace sauce, or even curry, depending on personal or regional preferences. It's a popular dish in Japanese homes and cafes, especially loved by kids and adults alike.

Omurice also became globally popular thanks to Japanese pop culture, anime, and viral videos showcasing the “tornado omelet” technique—where the egg is swirled into a delicate twist before gently unfolding over the rice. Whether served simple or gourmet-style, omurice offers the perfect bite of nostalgia, comfort, and flavor in one cozy meal.

INGREDIENTS

  • 2 cups cooked rice (preferably day-old)
  • 1/2 cup chopped onion
  • 1/2 cup diced chicken (or ham/shrimp)
  • 1/4 cup diced mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, corn)
  • 2 tablespoons ketchup (plus more for topping)
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce (optional for flavor depth)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tablespoon milk (optional, for fluffier eggs)
  • 1 tablespoon butter or cooking oil

STEPS

  1. Heat oil or butter in a pan over medium heat.
  2. Add chopped onions and cook until translucent.
  3. Add diced chicken (or your protein of choice) and cook until done.
  4. Add mixed vegetables and stir-fry for 2–3 minutes.
  5. Stir in the cooked rice and mix well.
  6. Add ketchup and soy sauce (optional), then season with salt and pepper. Stir to combine.
  7. Remove the rice mixture from the pan and shape it into an oval mound on a plate.
  8. In a clean pan, beat the eggs with a little milk and pour them in to make an omelet.
  9. Cook the eggs until just set but still soft in the center.
  10. Gently place the omelet over the rice mound or wrap it around the rice.
  11. Drizzle with extra ketchup or sauce on top, and serve warm.
